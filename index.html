Suparn Charitable Trust was established in 1997 by Shri. P.B.Kulkarni, former Executive Director of Reserve Bank of India and Chairman & Managing Director of Bank of Maharashtra.

Aims and Objectives:

  1. To render free financial assistance to-
    1. Poor and deserving students:
      • To pay for their school/college fees, and related educational expenses such as books and uniform.
    2. Elderly People (above 65 years ):
      • Who do not have adequate means to support themselves.
      • Whose children have deserted them or have no children.
  2. To lend interest free funds to unemployed youth (boys and girls) -
    • To support them in starting their own small scale business like Plumbing, Carpentry, Vegetables/Fruits stall, Newspaper distribution etc.
  3. To assist needy people in the event of a natural calamity.
  4. To undertake other activities as are conducive to the attainment of aforesaid objectives.

Administration and Funding:

The Trust is managed by 7 trustees with Shri. P.B.Kulkarni as the Settlor and the Maharashtra Executor and Trustee Company Pvt. Ltd. (a subsidiary of Bank of Maharashtra) as the Managing Trustee.
The Assistant Charity Commissioner, Pune, has registered the Trust under Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950.
The income-tax authorities have also registered the Trust under Sec 12A (a) of the Income Tax Act.
Donations to the Trust enjoy exemption under section 80G of the said Act.

The Trust is funded from donations primarily from friends, relatives, and charity oriented individuals (besides a substantial annual donation by the Settlor himself).
Atleast 85% of the income from the Trust is utilized for the objectives of the Trust.
Accounts are audited annually by the auditors and submitted to the Charity Commissioner and Income tax authorities.
