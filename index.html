Supports Underprivileged in our Society

Established by Shri. P.B. Kulkarni in 1997

Suparn Charitable Trust is a non-government organization (NGO) that supports the underprivileged in our society and provides them with a better platform.
With an aim to educate the poor students and cater the financial needs, Shri. P.B.Kulkarni, former Executive Director of Reserve Bank of India, and Chairman & Managing Director of Bank of Maharashtra, established Suparn Charitable Trust in 1997.

Our Objectives

We believe that providing financial assistance to the deprived will improve their quality of life and help them grab better opportunities too.

We provide financial assistance to the poor, deserving, and the elderly. This financial assistance includes the following:

  • Educational expenses, such as: school and college fees.
  • Stationery expenses, such as: books, and uniforms.
  • Basic amenities expenses.

We lend interest free funds to unemployed youth (both boys as well as girls).

  • We support them in starting their own small scale business like Plumbing, Carpentry, Vegetables/Fruits stall, Newspaper distribution etc.

We also assist needy people in the event of a natural calamity like earthquake or floods.

Our Board of Trustees

With establishment of the foundation, comes great responsibility. And, we have a dedicated management authority that handles all the fund-related and administrative tasks.
The Trust is managed by seven trustees. Shri. P.B.Kulkarni is the Settlor, Maharashtra Executor, and Managing Trustee of the Trustee Company Pvt. Ltd. (a subsidiary of Bank of Maharashtra).
Additionally, the auditors audit the accounts annually and submit it to the Charity Commissioner and Income tax authorities.

FUND SOURCES

The primary fund source of donations is from friends, relatives, and charity-oriented individuals.
Besides, the Settlor donates a substantial amount annually.
At least 85% of the income is utilized for achieving the objectives of the Trust.

TRUST REGISTERED ACTS

The Assistant Charity Commissioner, Pune, has registered the Trust under Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950.
The income-tax authorities have also registered the Trust under Sec 12A (a) of the Income Tax Act.
All the donations to the Trust are exempted under section 80G of the said Act.

The criteria to grant the scholarship are as follows:

  1. Parent's/guardian's annual income should not exceed Rs. 1,00,000
  2. Candidate should be qualified with a Secondary School Certificate (SSC).
  3. Candidate should secure minimum 60% marks in the examinations.
  4. Candidate should undertake courses that increase the chances of securing a job or setting up a business.
  5. Special circumstances, such as extreme poverty, special skills, disability etc. are given due consideration.
